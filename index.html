Research interests

Keywords: formal methods, verification, time Petri nets, temporal logic (LTL), model-checking, controller synthesis, parametric timed systems

My research has two main focuses:

  • During my PhD, under the supervision of Didier Lime and Olivier H. Roux (LS2N, Centrale Nantes), I worked on controller synthesis for parametric timed systems modelled using Time Petri Nets (TPNs). We developped a method to synthesise a strategy for a real-time controller , then we extended this method to parametric systems, in which time delays are parametrised , then we adapted an efficient computation of the state classes of a TPN, i.e. the state space of the net, to extend it to the parametric case.
  • During my master2 internship, I started a collaboration with François Laroussinie (IRIF, UPCité) and Arnaud Sangnier (DIBRIS, Universit\`a di Genova, Italy). We studied an extension of the temporal logic LTL to quantifications on set of states, called QLTL. In particular, we looked into its expressivity as well as the decidability and the complexity of the model-checking and the satisfaisability problems, using reductions to classical problems (tiling problems), as well as constructions using different types of automata on infinite words (alternating Büchi automata).
